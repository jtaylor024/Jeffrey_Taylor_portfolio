About me

Background & beyond the lab

Engineer, tinkerer, competitor

I'm a mechanical engineer with a master's degree and a bias toward building things that reach beyond the lab — deploying a robot that stands 12 feet tall, flying to Bolivia to set up a manufacturing line, or publishing research on mechanisms nobody had formalized before.

I'm drawn to problems at the intersection of physical design and systems thinking: how does this part move, how does this network fail, how does this product get made by real people in the real world?

Outside engineering I'm competitive. I serve as president and captain of the BYU Pickleball Club, where I also coach players at multiple skill levels. I've competed in and won intramural ultimate frisbee championships. I served a two-year mission for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in Argentina — which gave me fluent Spanish, deep cross-cultural fluency, and a genuine love for Latin America that showed up directly in my Bolivia fieldwork.
